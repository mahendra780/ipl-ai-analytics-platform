IPL MATCH ANALYSIS - INSIGHTS
This analysis is based on IPL match-level dataset using Python, Pandas, and Matplotlib.

1. TEAM PERFORMANCE
Mumbai Indians has the highest number of wins in IPL history, followed by Chennai Super Kings and Kolkata Knight Riders. This indicates strong long-term team performance and consistency.

2. CONSISTENT TEAMS
Chennai Super Kings shows consistent performance across seasons, maintaining a high win count despite playing fewer matches in some seasons.

3. MATCH RESULT ANALYSIS
Most IPL matches are won by wickets (chasing) rather than runs (defending). This suggests that teams generally prefer chasing targets.

4. CHASING ADVANTAGE
The higher number of wins by wickets indicates that chasing teams may have a strategic advantage, possibly due to better pitch understanding and target clarity.

5. TIE MATCHES
Very few matches end in a tie, indicating that most matches have a clear winner and competitive outcomes.

6. TOSS DECISION IMPACT
Teams often prefer to bowl first after winning the toss, supporting the trend of chasing being advantageous.

7. TOSS VS MATCH RESULT
Winning the toss does not always guarantee winning the match, but it can influence the strategy, especially in choosing to chase.

8. PLAYER PERFORMANCE
Certain players receive multiple "Player of the Match" awards, indicating their consistent impact in matches.

9. VENUE USAGE
Some venues host more matches than others, showing their importance in IPL scheduling.

10. DATA QUALITY OBSERVATION
Some teams have different names across seasons (e.g., Delhi Daredevils and Delhi Capitals), which required cleaning for accurate analysis.

11. PLAYER PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
Some players have received multiple Player of the Match awards, indicating their consistent match-winning performance and ability to perform under pressure.

12. SEASON ANALYSIS
The number of matches per season shows how the IPL has expanded over time, indicating growth in popularity and scale of the tournament.
